So, P is a subset of NP.

Polynomial time reducibility: L $\propto$ R

So, R is at least as hard as L. 
Note the asymetry

R is NP-hard if L $\propto$ R for all L in NP.
R in NP-complete if it is both NP-hard and in NP.
